The layer above the vendor stack

We work at the layer above the vendor stack. The 60/30/10 view says roughly 60% of most problems are traditional code and database work, 30% are rule-based logic, and 10% are genuine AI problems. Teams who skip that triage end up putting language models where Postgres would do the job faster, cheaper, and with cleaner audit trails.

60%Code & database
30%Rules
10%AI

Philips will deliver the imaging AI on the 10-year contract. The question is which handoffs in your clinical and operational pipeline actually need AI, which ones need a rule, and which ones are data-plumbing that should never have been automated with a model in the first place.

For pediatric systems the stakes on that triage are higher. Most clinical AI is trained on adult populations. Pediatric deployment needs a validation layer that the vendor does not own. A named internal framework, with audit trails that work for Joint Commission, Magnet, Most Wired, and state-level AI oversight, is an asset the Chief Strategy Officer and CIO will need regardless of which vendors are on the contract.

Ethics Engine

Ethics Engine is a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. It is the instrument we use to evaluate model behavior in regulated environments.

Paper on arXiv at arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742. Code at github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.
